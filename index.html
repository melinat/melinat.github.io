Melina Tsitsiklis

I am a Neuroscience PhD candidate in the Jacobs lab at Columbia University. I use intracranial eeg (iEEG) to understand how single neurons in the human medial temporal lobe code for space and memory. Prior to graduate school I studied Biological Engineering at MIT, and worked in the Tonegawa and Boyden labs.

Outside of research I love finding new ways to teach about the scientific process, and have both co-organized and volunteered for an outreach program that teaches middle school students about neuroscience, through Columbia University Neuroscience Outreach.

After my PhD I am interested in pursuing data-driven research to address fundamental questions in neuroscience, especially with applications to neurotechnology and healthcare.
